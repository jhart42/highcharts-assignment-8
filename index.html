Deaths Due to HIV/AIDS in Relation to Governmental Healthcare Spending

By Jacqueline Hart

Government spending on health care plays a huge role in preventing and treating diseases, including HIV infections. Not only does government spending decrease the chance of spreading infections, it also lowers the risk of sick patients being additionally infected by other life-threatening diseases, like tuberculosis (TB), due to a weakened immune system. It Is estimated that 60% of people living with HIV in South Africa are also co-infected with TB. (South Africa National AIDS Council, 2017). Overall, Government spending on healthcare is just one of many interesting criteria to see how countries are reacting, in part, to the HIV epidemic.
